# Screencast — 90-second product walkthrough

Six scenes, 90 seconds total at 30 fps (2,700 frames). Voice is editorial,
not announcer-cheerful — same Catholic Worker / Wendell Berry register as
the manifesto and the landing page.

Render: `cd remotion && npm run render -- Screencast out/screencast.mp4`

### Scene 1 — Cold open (0:00–0:10)

Mary runs Three Forks Dairy. Twelve cows. Thirty-eight shareholders. Her
old system was a binder, a group text, and a spreadsheet that hadn&apos;t
balanced since June. This is what she does in five minutes a day with
Communicare.

### Scene 2 — Sold-out tap (0:10–0:25)

Eight in the morning at the farmers market. The eggs are gone. She taps
once. The web store updates the same second. Members on the wait list get
a text the moment a share opens.

### Scene 3 — The SMS swap loop (0:25–0:45)

Monday morning. Every member gets this text. They can swap items, skip a
week, donate the share to the food pantry. Mary&apos;s roster updates in
real time. No phone calls. No inbox archaeology.

### Scene 4 — Tell the list (0:45–1:00)

A surprise wheel of cheddar this week. She taps "Tell the list." Eight in
stock. First eight to reply get one. The rest get put on the alert list
for next time.

### Scene 5 — The directory (1:00–1:15)

Across the country, a neighbor types in their ZIP. Communicare lists
every small farm in the directory — whether they use us or not. Mary&apos;s
farm shows up. The neighbor taps once. Mary wakes up to a new member.

### Scene 6 — Close (1:15–1:30)

Nine dollars a month. No setup fee. No contracts. The directory listing
is yours either way. Communicare-dot-farm. For the farms that feed us.
